# Technical Resume Masterclass — Getting Past the ATS and Into Interviews

Your resume has **six seconds** of human attention — if it even reaches a human. At most large companies, an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) parses and ranks your resume before any recruiter sees it. Understanding how ATS software works is the first step to landing interviews.

## How ATS Software Actually Works

An ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software that scans resumes for keywords, ranks candidates, and filters out applications that do not match the job description. Companies like Workday, Greenhouse, and Lever power hiring at over 95% of Fortune 500 firms.

The ATS does three things:

1. **Parses** your resume into structured fields (name, email, experience, skills)
2. **Matches** keywords from the job description against your content
3. **Ranks** you relative to other applicants

<FunFact>Over 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS software before a human ever reads them. A well-formatted, keyword-optimised resume dramatically increases your odds.</FunFact>

## The One-Page Rule

Unless you have 15+ years of experience, keep it to **one page**. Recruiters at Google, Amazon, and Meta have confirmed this in public talks repeatedly. One focused page beats two diluted pages every time.

## Resume Sections That Matter

### 1. Header
Name, email, phone, location (city only), LinkedIn URL, GitHub URL, portfolio link. No photo, no date of birth, no marital status.

### 2. Professional Summary (2-3 lines)
A concise pitch: your role, years of experience, key technologies, and a headline achievement.

> *"Senior Backend Engineer with 6 years building distributed systems in Java and Go. Led migration of payment service to event-driven architecture, reducing latency by 40% and saving £120K annually."*

### 3. Experience (the core)
Use the **XYZ formula** for each bullet: *Accomplished **X** by doing **Y**, resulting in **Z**.*

| Weak | Strong (XYZ) |
|------|--------------|
| "Worked on the search feature" | "Improved search relevance by 23% by implementing BM25 ranking, increasing user engagement by 15%" |
| "Responsible for CI/CD" | "Reduced deployment time from 45 min to 8 min by migrating to GitHub Actions with parallel test stages" |

### 4. Skills
List technologies honestly. Group them: **Languages**, **Frameworks**, **Cloud/DevOps**, **Data**. Match the exact terms from the job description — "Kubernetes" not "K8s" if the listing says "Kubernetes".

### 5. Education
Degree, university, graduation year. Add relevant coursework or thesis only if you are early-career.

## Action Verbs That Pass ATS Filters

Start every bullet with a strong verb: **Designed**, **Implemented**, **Migrated**, **Reduced**, **Orchestrated**, **Optimised**, **Automated**, **Scaled**, **Mentored**, **Delivered**.

Avoid weak openers: "Helped with", "Assisted in", "Was responsible for", "Participated in".

## Tailoring to Each Job Description

Copy the job description into a document. Highlight the **top 10 keywords** (technologies, methodologies, domain terms). Ensure at least 7 of those 10 appear naturally in your resume. This is not gaming the system — it is speaking the employer's language.

Create a **master resume** with all your experience, then tailor a copy for each application. Keep the master as your single source of truth and cut it down per role. A backend engineer applying for an ML platform role should emphasise data pipeline and infrastructure experience; the same person applying for a pure backend role should lead with API design and scalability.

## Common Mistakes That Kill Applications

- **Fancy templates with columns, tables, or graphics** — ATS cannot parse them. Use a single-column, clean layout.
- **Lying about skills** — technical interviews will expose this immediately, and it burns bridges permanently.
- **Including every technology you have ever touched** — list only what you can discuss confidently in an interview.
- **PDF with images or scanned text** — use a text-based PDF exported from a word processor.
- **Using a generic objective statement** — replace "seeking a challenging role" with a targeted professional summary that names the company and role.
- **Omitting dates** — interviewers notice and assume you are hiding employment gaps. Be transparent; gaps are normal.

## GitHub Profile Optimisation

Your GitHub is your living portfolio. Recruiters **do** check it.

- **Pin 4-6 best repositories** with clear README files (problem, solution, tech stack, how to run)
- **Write a profile README** (`username/username` repo) with a short bio and links
- **Maintain a contribution graph** — consistency matters more than intensity
- **Clean up old repos** — archive or make private anything embarrassing

## Portfolio Links and Personal Sites

If you have a personal site or portfolio, include it in your header. A well-structured portfolio page should contain:

- **Featured projects** with live demos or screenshots
- **A brief "about me"** section that matches your resume summary
- **Links to articles or talks** you have given
- Keep it fast-loading and mobile-friendly — recruiters often browse on phones

Use a custom domain (e.g., `janedoe.dev`) rather than a generic subdomain. It signals professionalism and attention to detail.

## LinkedIn Synergy

Your LinkedIn and resume should tell the **same story** with different depths. LinkedIn allows more detail, media, and recommendations. Ensure job titles, dates, and companies match exactly — discrepancies raise red flags.

Key LinkedIn optimisations:
- **Headline**: Use your target role and key technologies, not just your current title
- **About section**: Expand on your summary with a narrative tone and a call to action
- **Featured section**: Pin your best articles, talks, or project demos
- **Recommendations**: Request 2-3 from managers or senior colleagues who can speak to specific achievements

## Resume Checklist Before Submitting

- [ ] Single-column, no graphics, no tables
- [ ] Contact info complete (email, phone, LinkedIn, GitHub)
- [ ] Every bullet uses XYZ or action-verb-plus-impact format
- [ ] Skills section mirrors the job description keywords
- [ ] No typos (use a spell-checker and a human reviewer)
- [ ] Saved as text-based PDF
- [ ] One page (unless 15+ years experience)
